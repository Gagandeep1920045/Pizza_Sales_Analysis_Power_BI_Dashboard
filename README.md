# 🍕 Pizza Sales Analysis & Performance Dashboard
## 1. Short Description / Purpose
This project provides a comprehensive analysis of a year's worth of pizza sales data to identify key performance indicators (KPIs), consumer behavior patterns, and product performance. The goal is to provide a restaurant management team with data-driven insights to optimize inventory, improve marketing timing, and refine the menu.

## 2. Tech Stack
The dashboard was built using the following tools and technologies:  
•	📊 Power BI Desktop – Main data visualization platform used for report creation.  
•	📂 Power Query – Data transformation and cleaning layer for reshaping and preparing the data.  
•	🧠 DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – Used for calculated measures, dynamic visuals, and conditional logic.  
•	📝 Data Modeling – Relationships established among tables (pizzas,pizza types,order details,orders,dax created calendar date table) to enable cross-filtering and aggregation.  
•	📁 File Format – .pbix for development and .png for dashboard previews.  

## 3. Features / Highlights
### 📌 Business Problem  
The restaurant management lacked a centralized view of their sales performance. They were unable to pinpoint which days were most profitable, which pizza sizes drove the most revenue, or which menu items were underperforming and potentially wasting inventory costs. 
### Key Questions💡
Which products are bringing in the most money ?  
Which items are underperforming ?  
Which days and months are busiest ?  
What customer ordering patterns looks like ?  
### 📌 Goal of the Dashboard
The primary objective was to create an interactive tool that allows stakeholders to:
* Monitor daily and monthly sales trends.  
* Identify high-performing pizza categories and sizes.  
* Distinguish between "Best Sellers" and "Worst Sellers" based on Revenue, Quantity, and Total Orders.
### 📌 Walk-through of Key Visuals
* KPI Cards: Real-time tracking of Total Revenue ($817.86K), Total Orders (21,350), and Average Order Value ($38.31).  
* Sales Trends: A combination of bar and line charts highlighting peak order periods (Fridays and the month of July).  
* Category Analysis: Pie and Donut charts showing that the Classic Category and Large Size pizzas dominate the market share.  
* Performance Ranking: A dedicated "Best/Worst Seller" view using horizontal bar charts to compare the top and bottom 5 pizzas across three different metrics.
### 📌 Business Impact & Insights  
Key takeaways for the business:  
#### 1. Peak Demand Management: Sales peak significantly on Fridays and during the months of January and July. Management should ensure maximum staffing and inventory readiness during these windows to prevent order delays.  
#### 2. Inventory Optimization: Large pizzas contribute the maximum to total sales ($375.3K). Inventory and packaging supplies should be prioritized for this size category.
#### 3. Menu Engineering :

* The Winner: The Thai Chicken Pizza is the top revenue generator ($43K), despite not being the highest in quantity sold. This suggests high-profit margins.  

* The Underperformer: The Brie Carre Pizza consistently ranks at the bottom for revenue, quantity, and orders.  

* Recommendation: Consider a promotional campaign or "bundle deal" for the Brie Carre to increase movement, or consider replacing it with a new flavor profile similar to the "Classic" category, which holds 30% of the total sales volume.
#### 4. Customer Behavior : With an Average Order Value of $38.31, marketing efforts could focus on "Upselling" sides or drinks to push the average closer to the $45 revenue.
